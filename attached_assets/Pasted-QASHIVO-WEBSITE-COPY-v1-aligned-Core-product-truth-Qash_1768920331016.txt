FAQ (6)

1) What does “Attention” mean?
Attention is the list of receivables that need a human decision, like disputes, broken promises, unusual delays, or high-value risk. Everything else is handled automatically based on your rules.

2) How long does it take to see value?
Most teams see a usable forecast and working follow-ups within the first week. Setup time depends on your integrations and how much customization you want for templates and escalation rules.

3) What systems do you connect to?
Qashivo connects to common accounting systems and can link to email and CRM tools. If you don’t see your system listed, tell us what you use and we’ll confirm options.

4) Can we customize follow-ups and tone?
Yes. You can define templates, timing, and escalation paths to match your brand and customer relationships. Many teams start with a simple cadence and refine it based on results.

5) How does forecasting work?
Forecasts are driven by invoice-level receivables data and observed payment behavior. Qashivo updates projections automatically as invoices change, payments land, and promises are made or missed.

6) Is there an implementation fee?
Some plans include guided onboarding. If your setup needs custom workflows or integrations, we’ll scope that up front—no surprises.
