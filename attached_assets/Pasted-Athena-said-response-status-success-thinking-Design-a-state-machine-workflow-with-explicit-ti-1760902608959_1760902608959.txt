1) Canonical invoice states (single source of truth)

Issued → 2. Pre-Due Active → 3. Due → 4. Late → 5. PLDR Pending → 6. PLDR Active → 7. CCJ Awarded → 8. Enforcement Pending → 9. Enforcement Active → 10. Resolved
Parallel Pause overlays: Dispute, Promise to Pay (PTP), Payment Plan (PP). Pauses suspend timers and comms.

2) Timers and legal anchors (drive surfacing and automation)

T0 = Invoice Receipt. Start 30-day dispute window. If no dispute by T0+30, future disputes do not delay payment; statutory interest accrues after agreed term. Surface “Dispute window closing” at T0+25 to Exceptions.

Term cap: Cap payment terms at 60 days for UK B2B. Flag and surface any term >60 as Compliance Exception on creation. Roadmap: parameter to drop cap to 45 days if enacted later.

Default term reference: current framework still assumes 30-day default under the 1998 Act where applicable.

Statutory interest: once Late, compute interest at 8% + BoE base. Make it non-waivable in comms logic to reflect proposal; store accruals for reporting.

Reporting signal: for large debtors, track interest owed/paid for transparency and escalation logic.

3) Surfacing rules: when humans should see an invoice

Qashivo auto-handles routine nudges. Humans see only exceptions in dedicated queues.

Exceptions Queue (primary worklist)

Dispute window at T0+25 and still no acknowledgement. Action: confirm receipt, invite evidence upload to Dispute Portal.

Over-cap terms (>60d) detected on creation or ingestion. Action: propose amendment; flag to account owner.

Late + high value (configurable threshold), VIP customer, or broken PTP/PP. Action: manual call or supervised AI voice.

Aged ≥ term+15 with no promise and risk↑. Action: candidate for PLDR review.

Interest anomaly (interest suppressed or altered by debtor terms). Action: compliance check given mandatory proposal.

Large-company debtor with repeated late behavior (uses payment-practice data, when available). Action: escalate note for PLDR and reporting.

Approvals Hub

PLDR Approval: trigger when Late and any of: aged ≥ term+30, two broken promises, dispute rejected, or risk score ≥ threshold.

Enforcement Approval: trigger when CCJ Awarded + unpaid after grace (configurable 14 days).
Record approver, reason, and evidence bundle.

Pause Manager

PTP/PP start → suspend comms; set watchdogs: PTP due date, PP installment cadence. On failure, auto-re-age to appropriate stage by days late and behavior class.

Dispute start → route to Disputes Console. On Resolution, resume at correct stage; on Rejection, return to workflow at current age with confidence tags.

4) Channel and action matrix (static vs adaptive)

Static workflow: fixed cadence by bucket (e.g., Pre-Due: D-7 email, D-3 SMS, D-1 email; Late: L+1 email, L+5 SMS, L+10 call).

Adaptive workflow: risk-weighted path that adjusts cadence, channel mix, and tone using payment history and sentiment. Typical shift: faster call escalation for “high-risk, low-engagement” or softer approach for “formerly reliable, first-time late.” Research supports treating only exceptions while minimizing staff time.

Manual actions allowed when surfaced

Add call notes, attach evidence, propose or accept PTP/PP, trigger AI Voice (Retell), Email (SendGrid), SMS (Vonage), or set PLDR/Enforcement approvals.

8) MVP acceptance criteria

State machine: every invoice shows 1 active state and zero or more pause overlays.

Timers: dispute-window alerts at T0+25; auto-interest once late; cap-term flags at creation.

Surfacing: operators see only items in Exceptions, Approvals, Disputes, or Pause Manager.

PTP/PP: starting a plan pauses comms and timers; breach returns to correct stage by age and behavior.

PLDR/Enforcement: require approval with stored reason and pack export.

Reporting: interest owed and paid captured on each invoice; large-company flags visible.

Comms: Retell, Vonage, SendGrid actions idempotent with webhook confirmations.
