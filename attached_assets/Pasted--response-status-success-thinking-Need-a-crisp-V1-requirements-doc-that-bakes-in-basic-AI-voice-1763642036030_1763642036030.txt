Qashivo is an adaptive collections cockpit for Xero that tells you exactly who to chase today, by email, SMS, or AI voice, and what happened as a result.
Persona: Founder/MD, FD/VCFO, or credit controller.


Use case: “Clear today’s collections work in under 20 minutes, with better results than my spreadsheet + ad-hoc calls.”


Success for V1:
10 customers paying £295–£395/m.


At least 3 customers report a measurable DSO improvement or “cash in faster” story.



2. ICP and basic commercial shape

UK, Xero users.


B2B, invoice-based.


£500k–£5m revenue.


100+ invoices/month OR >£200k regularly outstanding.


Founding offer baked into product thinking:
90-day “reduce DSO by 10+ days or we extend free” promise.


Requires:


Clear baseline DSO at onboarding.


Basic before/after reporting.



3. Core V1 functional requirements
3.1 Xero integration & AR model (Must-have)
Requirements
OAuth connection to a single Xero org.


Pull:


Contacts/customers.


Sales invoices + credit notes.


Payments + allocations.


Refresh cadence:


Nightly automatic sync.


“Refresh now” button in UI.


Derived metrics:


Ageing buckets per invoice (0–30, 31–60, 61–90, 90+).


AR balance per customer.


Simple DSO (rolling 30/90 days – pick one formula and stick to it).


Why it sells: gives instant visibility and avoids “yet another spreadsheet”.

3.2 Action Centre (the heart of Qashivo)
Requirements
Single main screen listing actions, not invoices.


Each row shows:


Priority: High / Medium / Low (+ internal score).


Customer name.


Bundled invoices summary (count, total £, oldest days overdue).


Reason it’s here:
 e.g. “Newly overdue 7 days”, “PTP missed 3 days ago”, “High value >60 days.”


CTAs:


Send email


Send SMS


AI Voice Call


Snooze


Simple scoring logic:


Inputs: days overdue, amount, last contact date, PTP status.


Heuristics:


Missed PTP → High.


60 days & high £ → High.



Newly overdue <7 days & low £ → Low.


Filters:


By priority, by ageing band, by assigned owner (optional).


Default view shows High + Medium only (exceptions-first).


Why it sells: demos brilliantly; buyers see “today’s work” in one punchy list.

3.3 Debtor profile & timeline
Requirements
Click a row → right-side panel with:


Customer summary: total AR, total overdue, DSO for that customer, last payment date.


Invoice list: outstanding + recently paid, with due dates and amounts.


Activity timeline:


Email events (sent; optional open status if easy).


SMS events (sent/failed).


AI voice calls (status + short summary).


Manual notes (phone call logs, internal comments).


Why it sells: “single view of the customer” and proof of action if directors ask “what have you done to chase this?”.

3.4 Communications (email, SMS, AI voice)
3.4.1 Email (SendGrid)
Requirements
Connect to SendGrid using Qashivo’s account + per-tenant sender identities.


Small, fixed template library:


Friendly reminder (upcoming/just overdue).


Firm reminder (30+ days).


Pre-legal nudge (60–90+ days, but no legal threats).


Templates support:


Customer name / contact name.


Invoice list table (number, due date, amount).


Total overdue.


Payment link placeholder.


Flow:


From Action Centre → “Send email” opens pre-filled preview.


User can tweak text before sending.


Result stored on timeline with basic status (sent / failed; open if trivial).



3.4.2 SMS (Vonage)
Requirements
Outbound SMS only, via Vonage.


2–3 short templates:


“We’ve emailed you details of overdue invoices totaling £X with [Company]. Please check and advise payment date.”


“Quick reminder: you have £X overdue with [Company]. Reply with expected payment date or call [phone].”


From Action Centre → “Send SMS” with preview & edit.


Log to timeline with delivered/failed if available.



3.4.3 AI Voice (Retell) – Basic, but real
Requirements
One outbound AI agent (Retell), manual trigger only from the Action Centre.


Trigger: “AI Voice Call” button.


Opens modal showing phone number, customer/contact name, and a script summary.


Behaviour:


Short, polite script:


Identify self and purpose.


Confirm awareness of invoices.


Ask for payment date (PTP).


If can’t pay → capture reason.


If dispute → mark dispute and promise an email follow-up.


Hard rules:


No discounts.


No new bank details.


No legal threats.


Outcomes (from Retell via webhook) must be mapped into:


PTP + date.


No money / can’t pay + reason.


Dispute.


Wrong contact.


No answer / voicemail.


Qashivo must:


Log call on timeline (with short natural language summary).


Store outcome in structured fields.


Link recording URL for internal review (if enabled).


Feed outcome back to the scheduler:


PTP → reduce priority for a set time.


Dispute → move to “Needs review”.


No answer → queue email/SMS recommendation instead.


Why it sells: gives a strong “holy shit” moment in demos – “your AI is calling my customers for me” – without promising sci-fi.

3.5 Intelligence & scheduling (lightweight but real)
Requirements
Nightly job:


Recompute scores and priorities for all open customers based on:


New payments.


Promises to pay (and whether they’re met or missed).


Contact history (email/SMS/voice).


Generate tomorrow’s Action Centre list.


Cooldown logic:


Don’t suggest more than one outbound touch (any channel) within a given cool-off period unless a PTP was missed.


Feedback:


Thumbs up/down on each suggested action.


Store this for future model work (no live learning needed).



3.6 Dashboard & reporting
Requirements
Single dashboard page with:


Current DSO vs target.


Total AR and total overdue.


Overdue breakdown by band (0–30 / 31–60 / 61–90 / 90+).


“Coverage” metric:


% of overdue customers contacted in last 7 days.


“Risk highlight” tile:


“£X in 60+ days with no contact in last 14 days.”


90-day DSO trend (line chart) if data is available.


Simple “90-day DSO improvement” display used in sales:


“At onboarding: 64 days. Now: 52 days.”



3.7 Settings & admin (bare minimum)
Requirements
Tenant profile:


Business name, logo, base currency.


Target DSO.


Comms settings:


Default sender name + reply-to email.


Default SMS from name/number.


User management:


One owner, optionally a few standard users.


Email + password or magic link; no SSO in V1.



4. Explicit non-goals (V1)
To keep this shippable and sellable:
❌ No QuickBooks/Sage/NetSuite integrations (Xero only).


❌ No generic cashflow forecasting module (beyond simple “expected cash in” panel if you can squeeze it in).


❌ No rule-builder UI for scoring/scheduling – logic is code/config only.


❌ No inbound IVR / phone menu.


❌ No automatic batch dialling; every AI call is user-triggered.


❌ No deep analytics or ML-driven forecasts.


❌ No mobile apps (just responsive web).


❌ No granular roles/permissions or SSO.


These are Phase 2+ or investor-story bullets, not needed to land 10 customers.

“Here’s what’s changed since onboarding” – DSO trend and coverage metrics.
