🧭 Qashivo End-to-End Workflow (Customer First → Accounting Sync)
🧩 Core Principle

All customers (debtors) are first onboarded and risk-checked inside Qashivo.
Only approved and insured clients are pushed into the accounting system for invoicing.
This ensures credit discipline, insurance compliance, and finance eligibility from day one.

1️⃣ Add New Customer in Qashivo

User clicks “Add Client”.

Inputs company name, registration/VAT number, email, and payment terms.

Qashivo instantly calls Credit Check / Insurance Partner API.

Credit Partner Response

Returns:

Credit Rating (A–E)

Suggested Credit Limit (£)

Free Base Cover Limit (£ per invoice)

Optional Full Cover Premium (£)

Initial Insurance Policy ID

🧠 Example:

“Client: ACME Ltd — Credit Rating B — Credit Limit £5,000 — Free Cover £2,500 — Optional Full Cover £19.80.”

✅ Action:
Qashivo stores this as a new Customer Record with:

Credit score + cover level

Behavioural risk placeholder (no history yet)

Approval status = Pending

2️⃣ User Reviews & Approves Customer

User sees a summary card:

Risk Level (Low / Medium / High)

Credit Limit

Free Base Cover

Upgrade Option

One click “Approve & Sync to Xero” button.

Behind the Scenes

Qashivo calls Xero (or Sage/QuickBooks) API → creates new Customer Contact.

Injects:

Credit Limit

Insurance Reference

Risk Score metadata (custom field)

✅ Action:
Customer now live in both systems, ready for invoice creation in Xero.
Qashivo keeps the master risk data.

3️⃣ Invoice Created in Xero

User (or their normal process) raises an invoice to that client in Xero.

Qashivo’s connector listens via webhook for new invoice events.

✅ Action:
Invoice imported automatically into Qashivo with:

Client ID

Amount / Due Date

Payment Terms

Insurance metadata attached (from stored profile)

4️⃣ Automatic Insurance Activation

Qashivo immediately attaches base insurance cover (e.g. £2,500 free).

If invoice exceeds limit, user sees:

“Extend to 100% cover for £19.80.”

Full cover premium adjusts dynamically based on live risk model.

🛡️ Status Examples:

Invoice	Amount	Free Cover	Full Cover	Status
INV-1043	£2,000	£2,000	£0	Fully Covered (Free)
INV-1044	£5,000	£2,500	£19.80	Partial – Upgrade Available
5️⃣ AI Credit-Control Automation

Once active, invoice enters AI communications cycle (email, SMS, voice).

System tracks sentiment and behaviour: tone, response time, disputes.

Payment behaviour data updates client’s Q-Score weekly.

Insurer and lender APIs receive anonymised updates for dynamic pricing.

6️⃣ Invoice Finance Option

Any active, insured invoice shows “Get Paid Now” button.

Clicking triggers Finance Partner API offer (e.g., 95 % advance, £40 fee).

If accepted:

Funds credited into Qashivo Wallet (not bank).

Invoice tagged Financed.

💬 Voice Agent Example:

“We can advance £4,750 today directly into your Qashivo Wallet.
When your customer pays, we’ll settle it automatically.”

7️⃣ Payment & Wallet Auto-Offset

Debtor pays invoice into Qashivo Wallet virtual account.

System auto-reconciles:

Deducts advance + finance fee.

Releases residual balance to user’s bank.

If insured invoice defaults → insurer payout lands in Wallet and clears balance.

8️⃣ Feedback Loop & Dynamic Adjustment

Payment timeliness updates Q-Score.

Insurer adjusts base-cover limit & full-cover premium.

Finance partner refines lending rate.

Qashivo dashboard updates with:

Credit-checked clients %

Insured invoice value

Financed invoice value

Behavioural improvement trend

          ↓                               ↓

⚖️ How Qashivo Differs from Traditional Factoring
Aspect	Traditional Factoring	Qashivo Embedded Finance Model
Ownership Model	The factor buys the receivable — they legally own it until payment.	The SME retains ownership; Qashivo simply finances and insures the cash flow via partners.
Customer Experience	Opaque, high-friction onboarding. Credit checks, legal assignments, debtor notifications.	Seamless: invoice stays in Xero; credit check + insurance handled in-app. No legal reassignment.
Integration	Separate platform; manual upload of invoices or CSV imports.	Native, API-driven sync with Xero/Sage/QuickBooks. Instant eligibility decisions.
Risk Model	Blanket facility: fixed fees, tied to all invoices.	Dynamic, invoice-by-invoice risk pricing. Free base cover + optional full cover.
Insurance	Often bundled; hidden in factoring fee (2–4%).	Transparent: free insurance base tier, smart upgrade option; insurer brand visible.
Flexibility	You must sell all invoices in a facility.	Finance only those you choose. Full control at invoice level.
Funding Flow	Factor pays into your bank; holds control over debtor payments.	Funds route through Qashivo Wallet. Automatic offset when paid; no change to customer payment process.
Customer Relationship	Debtor often notified of assignment — can strain client relationships.	Debtor still pays your existing account; no “third party collection” stigma.
Data Intelligence	Static underwriting, based on financials.	AI-driven, dynamic risk scores based on behavioural payment patterns and sentiment.
Target Market	Mid-market, creditworthy firms with consistent turnover (£1M+).	SMEs (£100K–£5M) who want selective, tech-driven protection and cash-flow access.
Exit Value	Low-multiple, commoditised lending.	High-multiple SaaS + embedded finance platform (IP + recurring income).
💎 In Short

Qashivo isn’t a factor — it’s an AI Receivables Layer that connects credit control, insurance, and finance into one dynamic ecosystem.

Factoring = “We buy your invoices.”

Qashivo = “We protect and accelerate your invoices.”

That’s the philosophical difference investors love — it’s software-first, not balance-sheet-first.
