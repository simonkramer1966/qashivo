Implement an interactive “Email Demo: Two-way Outcomes” module on the demo page directly under the AI Voice call demo.

Goal
Demonstrate Qashivo’s supervised autonomy loop in ~60–90 seconds:
Plan → Approve → Send → Receive reply → Detect outcome → Update plan + cash-in confidence.
This is a deterministic demo (no randomness) with seeded invoices and scripted replies. No external integrations.

Component
- Build a single self-contained component: <EmailOutcomeDemo />
- Render it on the demo page directly beneath the AI Voice call demo.
- Keep styling consistent with Qashivo: clean, calm, minimal, no heavy cards; use whitespace, subtle dividers, and clear typography.

Flow UI (left-to-right)
Build a single-screen layout with a calm, guided flow:

1) Select invoice (dropdown)
   - Shows: Customer name + invoice number + amount + overdue label
   - Also show a small inline label next to the amount:
     “Confidence: High / Medium / Low” (based on seeded confidenceBand)

2) Generate reminder button
   - Button label: “Generate reminder”
   - Clicking generates a deterministic subject/body (read-only) that references the chosen invoice number, amount, and due date.
   - The message should be polite and relationship-preserving.

3) Approval step (supervised autonomy)
   - Buttons:
     Primary: “Approve & send”
     Secondary: “Edit message” (unlocks the subject/body fields)
     Tertiary: “Decline”
   - If Declined: log an audit event and stop the flow (but allow switching invoice to restart).
   - On Approve & send: show “Sent ✓” with a timestamp and lock the message again.

4) Simulate customer reply (appears only after send)
   Show 4 buttons:
   - “Promise to pay”
   - “Request more time”
   - “Dispute”
   - “Ambiguous”

Conversation Thread Panel
- Show a thread view with:
  - Outbound email (from “Your company” to debtor) with subject/body + timestamp
  - Inbound reply (from debtor) inserted when simulated
- Keep it visually lightweight: simple rows, channel icon (email), timestamps, subtle separators.

Intent Detection + Outcome Card
After a reply is simulated:
- Show an interim state: “Interpreting reply…” (brief loading state)
- Then render an Outcome Card showing:
  - outcomeType: PROMISE_TO_PAY | REQUEST_TIME | DISPUTE | AMBIGUOUS
  - extracted details (when applicable): promisedDate, promisedAmount, reason
  - confidence score (0–1)
  - a “Needs review” badge when:
    - confidence < 0.6 OR
    - outcomeType is AMBIGUOUS
  - recommendedNextStep (plain English)
- The outcome must be deterministic for each scripted reply.

Outcome Effects (apply immediately after interpretation)
Implement these exact behaviours:

1) PROMISE_TO_PAY
   - Plan: “Paused until {promisedDate}” and schedule follow-up next business day after promisedDate
   - Forecast: move the invoice amount into “High confidence” on promisedDate
   - Confidence label animates from previous to “High”
   - Briefly flash microcopy: “Forecast updated”

2) REQUEST_TIME
   - Plan: move invoice to “Attention” with note “Payment plan requested”
   - Forecast: set “Medium confidence” and push expected date +14 days (until reviewed)
   - Confidence label animates to “Medium”
   - Briefly flash: “Forecast updated”

3) DISPUTE
   - Plan: stop automation and move invoice to “Attention” with note “Dispute raised”
   - Forecast: set to “Low confidence” OR remove from expected cash until resolved (choose one and implement consistently)
   - Confidence label animates to “Low”
   - Briefly flash: “Forecast updated”

4) AMBIGUOUS
   - Plan: “Needs review” with suggested follow-up question: “Thanks — can you confirm the expected payment date for this invoice?”
   - Forecast: leave unchanged, but mark as uncertain in the UI
   - Confidence label remains as-is
   - Show “Needs review” badge regardless of confidence score
