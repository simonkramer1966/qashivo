✅ Qashivo Debtor Record — World-Class Requirements

A debtor record must act as the single source of truth for understanding the customer, their payment behaviour, their promises, and how Qashivo should chase them. Nothing should require digging through inboxes, spreadsheets, or phone logs.

Below is the recommended complete structure:

1️⃣ Core Debtor Identity & Contact Details

Purpose: Ensure we always know who we are contacting and how they want to be contacted.

Business name (from Xero)

Trading name / group / parent entity (optional but valuable)

Billing contact(s):

Primary email

Backup email

Mobile / landline

Contact role (e.g., Owner, Accounts Payable, Bookkeeper)

Additional contacts added in Qashivo (multi-contact support)

Preferred channel(s): Email / SMS / Voice

Address & jurisdiction (for any legal escalation later)

Time zone (relevant for call/SMS windows)

Contact validation flags (invalid email, unreachable number, etc.)

2️⃣ Engagement & AI Contact Strategy

Purpose: Tailor communications based on debtor behaviour and compliance with past outreach.

AI Contact Approach Profile:
E.g.: Soft-touch, Firm but polite, High-value formal, Escalation-ready.
(V1 could be simple: Friendly / Standard / Firm.)

Channel hierarchy:
Which channels to try first → last (Email → SMS → AI Voice → Manual call).

Cadence settings:
Days between reminders, cooling-off periods, PTP adherence delays.

Restrictions / preferences:

Don’t call before/after specific times

Don’t SMS

Legal entity only accepts written notices

Internal do/don’t contact notes

3️⃣ VIP, Exceptions & Compliance Flags

Purpose: Operational guardrails so AI does not behave incorrectly.

VIP flag (no automated steps)

Opt-out of automation (email/SMS/voice or selective opt-outs)

Requires owner approval before escalation

Bad debt / Watchlist / Under dispute

Do-not-contact reasons (e.g., litigation, insolvency)

4️⃣ Ownership & Internal Assignment

Purpose: Make it clear who in Qashivo owns this debtor relationship.

Assigned Qashivo user (credit controller / CFO / founder)

Secondary owner or backup owner

Routing rules (assign by ageing, value, region, etc. — future)

5️⃣ Financial Profile

Purpose: See the entire debtor’s value and ageing at a glance.

AR Balance: total outstanding, overdue, and by ageing bucket

Total open invoices and count

High-value invoice flag

Credit limit (if stored in Xero)

Average invoice value

Days Overdue for oldest invoice

Weighted overdue average (helps risk scoring)

6️⃣ Debtor-Level Key Metrics

Purpose: Predict risk and prioritise intelligently.

Current DSO (customer-specific)

Rolling 90-day DSO trend

Payment behaviour score:

Always pays late

Pays on PTP

Unreliable

First-time late

Risk Score (derived):

Inputs: outstanding amount, ageing, past disputes, broken PTPs, missed contacts

Broken Promise Count

Dispute frequency

% of invoices paid on time historically

7️⃣ Invoices & Transaction History

Purpose: Instant clarity—nothing beats seeing exactly what is owed.

Full invoice list:

Paid

Unpaid

Overdue

Recently allocated payments

Invoice metadata:
Invoice number, issue date, due date, amount, ageing, last chase, dispute flag.

Credit notes, write-offs and adjustments

8️⃣ Communications Timeline / Activity Log

Purpose: Treat this like a “case file” — everything must be searchable and audit-ready.

Includes every touch:

Outbound email (with template used, status, opens if available)

Outbound SMS (status: delivered/failed)

AI Voice Calls

Summary of call

Outcome (PTP, dispute, no answer…)

Link to recording

Manual calls / notes

Inbound emails (future V2)

Inbound SMS replies (V2)

Inbound calls (V2)

All entries should be searchable by text and filterable by type.

9️⃣ Promises-To-Pay (PTP) Management

Purpose: This is the beating heart of effective collections.

PTP status: Active / Completed / Missed

PTP amount

PTP date expected

Reason for delay (if reported)

History of PTPs (completed vs missed)

This directly feeds the scoring engine.

🔟 Dispute Tracking

Purpose: Distinguish “won’t pay” from “can’t pay” vs “dispute”.

Dispute flag (active/not active)

Dispute reason

Wrong amount

Missing PO

Service issue

Quality complaint

Internal AP delays

Evidence attachments (optional)

Date raised

Resolution status & notes

1️⃣1️⃣ Scheduling & Next Action

Purpose: The user should instantly see the next step without thinking.

Next recommended action (email/SMS/AI call)

Priority level (High/Med/Low + score)

Last touch date & channel

Cooldown period remaining

Escalation trigger date

Last update to score

Reason they are appearing in Action Centre
(e.g., “Missed PTP”, “60+ days”, “High value newly overdue”)

1️⃣2️⃣ Internal Tags & Segmentation

Purpose: Helps large clients or partners manage portfolios.

Industry / sector

Region

Account type (retainer, subscription, one-off)

Partner-managed / accountant-managed flag

Custom tags (“difficult payer”, “seasonal”, “VIP supplier”, etc.)

1️⃣3️⃣ Attachments & Reference Files

Purpose: Unified evidence store.

Contracts

Statements

Previous legal letters

Credit application forms

Buyer/supplier correspondence (future via email ingestion)

Payment Behaviour Classifier (simple ML)

Auto-label customers as:

Always on time

Slightly late

Chronically late

Only pays after chasing

Likelihood of Payment Score

A simple probability estimate:

High likelihood

Medium

Low

Preferred Payment Method + Payment Links

Store Stripe/GoCardless references where relevant.

Legal Readiness Flag

If they breach PTP twice + 60+ days → mark as escalation candidate.

✅ Summary: What Qashivo Needs on Every Debtor Record

A world-class debtor record is:

A complete customer-level dossier combining commercial identity, financial exposure, behavioural intelligence, communications history, promises, disputes, and automation settings—so that every chase, every call, and every recommendation is informed and effective.

This record becomes the brain of the Action Centre, driving prioritisation, messaging tone, AI call strategy, and risk scores.
