### 3.12 Additional Copy Changes — Multi-Channel, Conversational Persona, Human Handback

**Four positioning principles to apply across all pages:**

**PRINCIPLE 1 — Tone down "AI" language:**
Replace "AI" with "Qashivo" wherever possible. Use "AI" sparingly — once or twice per page max, only in high-level positioning (e.g., "AI-powered" in the hero tagline for SEO). Business owners want to know it works, not how it works.

**PRINCIPLE 2 — Multi-channel is about finding what works for each debtor:**
Qashivo uses email, SMS, and automated voice calls. The message is: "Qashivo finds the channel each debtor responds to fastest — surfacing payment intent or issues sooner, leading to faster payment and better debtor relationships."

**PRINCIPLE 3 — Two-way conversational communications, not one-way blasts:**
This is a major differentiator. Emails and SMS are fully two-way — Qashivo doesn't just send chase emails and hope for the best. It reads replies, understands intent, and responds conversationally within strict guardrails. The user creates a persona (name, title, tone of voice) and Qashivo communicates as that persona — like having another member of your credit control team. Two-way voice calls are coming soon. Frame this as: "Qashivo isn't a mail merge. It's a teammate."

**PRINCIPLE 4 — Human handback for edge cases:**
Disputes, complaints, inability to pay, payment plan requests, and complex situations are all escalated back to a human. Qashivo handles the routine conversational chasing — your people handle the relationships that need a human touch.

## 7. Brand Voice & Positioning

Qashivo targets UK SMEs and mid-market businesses (£500k–£20m turnover). The buyer is a business owner, managing director, or finance director.

**Tone:** Confident, direct, professional, human. Like a sharp finance professional explaining something clearly — not a corporate bank being stiff, and not a startup trying to be clever.

### "AI" Language Rule
Use "AI" very sparingly — maximum once or twice per page, only in high-level positioning. Everywhere else, say "Qashivo" instead of "AI". The product should feel like a trusted teammate, not an experiment.

### The "Teammate" Positioning
Qashivo is positioned as "like another member of your team." The user creates a persona (name, title, tone) and Qashivo communicates as that persona. This is central to the brand — Qashivo isn't a tool that sends emails. It's a credit controller that has conversations on your behalf.

### Two-Way Conversational Messaging
Emails and SMS are fully two-way. Qashivo doesn't just send and hope — it reads replies, understands intent (promises to pay, disputes, queries, hardship), and responds conversationally within strict guardrails. This is a major differentiator vs competitors who send one-way template emails. Two-way automated voice calls are coming soon — can be mentioned as "coming soon" but not as a current feature.

### Multi-Channel Positioning
Qashivo communicates via email, SMS, and automated voice calls. The message is: "Qashivo finds the channel each debtor responds to fastest — surfacing payment intent or issues sooner, leading to faster payment and better relationships." Frame multi-channel as intelligent and relationship-preserving, not aggressive.

### Human Handback Messaging
Always make clear that edge cases — disputes, complaints, payment plans, hardship, inability to pay — are escalated back to a human. Position this as: "Qashivo handles the routine conversations; your people handle the relationships." This is a trust signal, not a limitation.

### Do:
- Say "Qashivo" where you'd normally say "the AI"
- Frame communications as "two-way conversations" not "automated emails"
- Emphasise persona creation — "your credit controller, your rules"
- Frame multi-channel as finding what works for each debtor
- Emphasise human handback for edge cases
- Use "automated" not "AI-powered" for voice calls
- Use real outcome numbers ("15-day DSO reduction", "89% of routine chases handled automatically")

### Don't:
- Use "AI" in every feature description
- Frame multi-channel as aggressive ("we chase on every channel")
- Say "mail merge" or anything that suggests one-way templated emails — Qashivo has conversations
- Use enterprise jargon ("deploy", "infrastructure", "tactical intelligence")
- Reference WhatsApp or channels not yet built
- Claim two-way voice is live — it's "coming soon"
- Say "autonomous" when "automatic" feels less intimidating
- Suggest the system handles everything — always note human handback
